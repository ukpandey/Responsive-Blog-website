One Piece: The Yonkos' 10 Worst Crimes, Ranked

Author - Eren Yeager
07 January. 6 min read

10. Whitebeard Recruited A Slave Trader Into His Family

The squid-wearing Karma assisted Whitebeard's forces in the battle for Marineford. At first, he blended in as one of the series' unusually designed characters and did not play a particularly notable role. However, the Wano arc revealed the extent of his depravity. Karma first encountered Whitebeard when attempting to kidnap Kozuki Toki, Oden's wife. He only stopped when the emperor arrived personally. Given that Whitebeard later included Karma in his family, he implicitly condoned his actions.

9. Big Mom Accidentally Killed Her Entire Adoptive Family

Having been abandoned by her biological parents, Charlotte Linlin, or Big Mom, as she'd later become known, was adopted by Carmel. She was the only woman with the time and patience to satiate Big Mom's appetite, coming time and again to her rescue when necessary. However, Big Mom's gluttony eventually got the better of her, and she accidentally devoured Carmel amidst one of her infamous hunger pangs. 8. Blackbeard Is Indirectly Responsible For Ace's Death

While Akainu may have struck the killing blow, Blackbeard was ultimately responsible for Ace's death. He delivered the young man into the World Government's custody in exchange for the office of Warlord. Blackbeard's treachery was heinous for a number of reasons. Not only was Ace a former "family" member under Whitebeard's crew, but he also knew it would result in the Marineford conflict. Such dramatic global destabilization terrified populations across the world and resulted in territories previously belonging to Whitebeard coming under savage attack. To Blackbeard's credit, however, Ace attacked him first. Aokiji's intervention.

7. Blackbeard Murdered Thatch & Stole His Devil Fruit

The Whitebeard pirates had a policy stating that whoever found a devil fruit first would be able to claim it. Unfortunately for Blackbeard, Thatch discovered the darkness fruit he spent years looking for. Nonetheless, Blackbeard would not be deterred. He exploited Thatch's trust to murder him and steal his power for himself. While Blackbeard realized that there was no going back to the Whitebeard Pirates, he didn't care. The villain had already become one of the most powerful fighters in the Grand Line; it only took the assassination of a comrade.

6. Big Mom Attempted To Usurp The Kingdom Of Germa

At first, the wedding Big Mom arranged between Pudding and Sanji seemed like a celebration for the ages. She invited guests from across the world to attend, with even the prestigious Big News Morgan making an appearance. However, everything was a ruse to usurp Germa 66's vast technology. The second Pudding was to kiss Sanji, Big Mom's pirates attacked Vinsmoke Judge and his children. Were it not for Luffy's unexpected intervention, the acquisition of Germa 66's cloning technology would've occurred without incident.

5. Kaido Permitted Queen To Use Vile Bioweapons

Queen was one of Kaido's most esteemed subordinates and was a critical member of his army. In addition to monitoring the brutal slave camp of Udon, he created vile bio-weapons that decimated his master's adversaries.However, given the virulent nature of Queen's plagues, it often spread to allies as well. The toxins proved so profoundly fatal that they even hospitalized Luffy, who had already developed strong antibodies against poisons from his fight with Magellan. Kaido's permissiveness of Queen's weapons suggests that he has no moral code when annihilating his enemies.

4. Kaido Helped To Install Orochi As The Evil Shogun Of Wano

Orochi was the despotic shogun of Wano for decades. Under his rule, dissent was crushed, history was erased, and famine spread like wildfire. Though his personal guard provided a layer of defense, he primarily used the Beast Pirates for serious threats. Considering the atrocities that Kaido's support enabled, he is directly responsible for Orochi's crimes. Since the shogun fed a starving village defective SMILE fruits to stop their complaining, there is little possibility that Kaido or Orochi are redeemable characters.

3. Big Mom Exploited Thousands For Their Lifespans

Living on Whole Cake Island came with a high price. Every year, Big Mom forced its residents to pay tribute in order to keep their homes and livelihoods. The cost was worse than money; instead, she took a fraction of their total lifespans. Some consider this trade to be fair. After all, they technically benefit from her protection as a Yonko. However, considering that their home may still be destroyed any time Big Mom has a hunger attack, their lives are devoid of justice or a sense of security.

2. Kaido Instructed Jack To Destroy Zou

Jack the Drought was a monstrous pirate and one of the three subordinates directly under Kaido's charge. He instructed him to find Raizo by any means necessary, which resulted in the destruction of Zou. When the Beast Pirates attacked, hundreds of innocent minks were put in danger,] their livelihoods completely destroyed. In the end, Jack was so desperate to fulfill his mission that he even attacked Zunesha directly in hopes of killing everyone in the city. Fortunately, Momonosuke commanded the great beast to defend itself and sink Jack's vessel to the bottom of the sea.

1. Blackbeard Nearly Killed Everyone At The End Of The Marineford Fight

The climax of the Marineford arc revealed how evil Blackbeard actually was. In addition to ambushing and killing Whitebeard, he used the man's stolen power to attack the remaining pirates and the World Government - both he had gleefully betrayed. By this point, Blackbeard's power increased so dramatically that he became one of the few pirates with a bounty of more than one billion berries. After crushing the remnants of the Whitebeard pirates, he was also named a Yonko. Perhaps the most jarring aspect of Blackbeard's villainy is that, unlike Kaido or Big Mom, he has yet to be defeated.
